Sofya Sellak

I am a Ph.D. student in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Texas at El Paso, researching robotic dexterous manipulation. I hold a B.S. in General Engineering with a minor in AI from Al Akhawayn University. My academic journey includes exchange programs in Malaysia and the Czech Republic, and my research spans robotics, computer vision, and intelligent systems.

I've worked as a Research Intern at AI Movement in Morocco, where I developed visual navigation for mobile robots. I also served as an R&D consultant at Leyton, drafting technical reports for R&D tax credit claims. My passion lies in building robotic systems that safely and intelligently interact with dynamic environments.

Beyond research, I love mentoring, building robots, and exploring interdisciplinary innovations that bridge AI and real-world impact.
